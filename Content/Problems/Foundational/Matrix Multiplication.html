Matrix Multiplication

Problem Description

Given two matrices \( A \) of size \( m \times n \) and \( B \) of size \( n \times p \) with real number entries, compute the product matrix \( C = A \cdot B \), where \( C \) is an \( m \times p \) matrix and each element \( c_{ij} \) is defined as: \[ c_{ij} = \sum_{k=1}^{n} a_{ik} \cdot b_{kj} \]

Input

Two matrices:
- Matrix \( A \) with dimensions \( m \times n \)
- Matrix \( B \) with dimensions \( n \times p \)

Output

Matrix \( C \) with dimensions \( m \times p \), where each entry is the dot product of the corresponding row of \( A \) and column of \( B \).

Motivation/Applications

Matrix multiplication is a core operation in linear algebra and has applications in computer graphics, scientific simulations, machine learning (e.g., neural networks), and solving systems of linear equations. Efficient matrix multiplication is essential in both theoretical and practical computing.

Examples

Example 1
Input:
\( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 4 & 5 & 6 \\ 7 & 8 & 9 \end{bmatrix} \), \( B = \begin{bmatrix} 9 & 8 & 7 \\ 6 & 5 & 4 \\ 3 & 2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \)

Computation: Here are the computations of each entry of \(A\times B\):
  • \((1,1): 1\cdot9 + 2\cdot6 + 3\cdot3 = 9 + 12 + 9 = 30\)
  • \((1,2): 1\cdot8 + 2\cdot5 + 3\cdot2 = 8 + 10 + 6 = 24\)
  • \((1,3): 1\cdot7 + 2\cdot4 + 3\cdot1 = 7 + 8 + 3 = 18\)
  • \((2,1): 4\cdot9 + 5\cdot6 + 6\cdot3 = 36 + 30 + 18 = 84\)
  • \((2,2): 4\cdot8 + 5\cdot5 + 6\cdot2 = 32 + 25 + 12 = 69\)
  • \((2,3): 4\cdot7 + 5\cdot4 + 6\cdot1 = 28 + 20 + 6 = 54\)
  • \((3,1): 7\cdot9 + 8\cdot6 + 9\cdot3 = 63 + 48 + 27 = 138\)
  • \((3,2): 7\cdot8 + 8\cdot5 + 9\cdot2 = 56 + 40 + 18 = 114\)
  • \((3,3): 7\cdot7 + 8\cdot4 + 9\cdot1 = 49 + 32 + 9 = 90\)
Output:
\( A \times B = \begin{bmatrix} 30 & 24 & 18 \\ 84 & 69 & 54 \\ 138 & 114 & 90 \end{bmatrix} \)

Example 2
Input:
\( A = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & 2\\ -1 & 3 & 1\end{bmatrix} \), \( B = \begin{bmatrix}3 & 1\\ 2 & 1\\ 1 & 0\end{bmatrix} \)

Computation: Here are the computations of each entry of \(A\times B\):
  • \((1,1): 1\cdot3 + 0\cdot2 + 2\cdot1 = 3 + 0 + 2 = 5\)
  • \((1,2): 1\cdot1 + 0\cdot1 + 2\cdot0 = 1 + 0 + 0 = 1\)
  • \((2,1): -1\cdot3 + 3\cdot2 + 1\cdot1 = -3 + 6 + 1 = 4\)
  • \((2,2): -1\cdot1 + 3\cdot1 + 1\cdot0 = -1 + 3 + 0 = 2\)
Output:
\( A\times B = \begin{bmatrix}5 & 1\\ 4 & 2\end{bmatrix} \)
